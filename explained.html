What's on Your Berkeley Property Tax Bill?

A complete guide to every line item on a typical Alameda County tax invoice for a Berkeley residential property

Your Berkeley property tax bill isn't just one tax — it's a stack of 20+ separate levies from the county, city, school district, and special districts. Some are based on your home's assessed value (the Prop 13 value the county has on record). Others are based on the square footage of your building or lot. This page explains each one.

Ad Valorem Taxes (Based on Assessed Value)

These are percentage-based taxes applied to your county-assessed property value. Under Prop 13, your assessed value can only increase by 2% per year (Berkeley applies the full 2% every year) unless the property changes hands, at which point it resets to market value.

Example: $800,000 Assessed Value
$800,000 × 1.2323% = $9,858 / year in ad valorem taxes
Prop 13 annual increase: $800,000 × 2% × 1.2323% = $197 more each year
City of Berkeley Parcel Taxes (Per Square Foot of Building)

These are voter-approved special taxes charged per square foot of building improvements. Several include a built-in 6.44% annual escalator. Rates shown are FY 2025–26 residential rates.

The 6.44% escalator means these taxes grow faster than inflation. For the five measures with the escalator, the combined increase is approximately $0.0534 / sq ft per year — about $75/year for a 1,400 sq ft home, even without any new measures passing.
